zadar 🇭🇷

Small town on the Dalmatian coast of Croatia, bathed by the Adriatic Ocean.

It had significant Italian influence, especially during the rule of the Republic of Venice, which began in 1202 after the Fourth Crusade and lasted until the 18th century. During this period, Zadar flourished as an important commercial and cultural center, with architecture and art strongly influenced by Venice.

After the decline of the Republic of Venice in 1797, the city was integrated into the Austro-Hungarian Empire but was ceded to Italy by the Treaty of Rapallo in 1920. During World War II, Zadar was severely damaged, and after the war, it was integrated into Yugoslavia, eventually becoming part of independent Croatia in 1991.

20/05 - Arrival in Zadar

This was our second trip to Croatia, but this time we were accompanied by my mother, Sidia, on her first trip to Europe.

Actually, she had arrived about three hours ago, and we were already heading on the trip to Zadar.

Between buses, trains, and a plane, we arrived in Zadar at 8:25 in the morning. We planned to take a local bus upon arrival but ended up taking a somewhat suspicious transport from the airport. It was a private van that called for people who were going to take the bus.

Fortunately, everything went well, and we arrived at our hotel, Apartments Jadera, without any issues.

After having a meal at the hotel, we set off for the island of Preko using a ferry. On the island, we explored the:

  • Historic Center;
  • Strolled along the seaside promenade (Šetnja uz obalu);
  • Visited NavAdriatic;
  • The Church of Our Lady of the Rosary (Crkva Gospa od Ružarija);
  • and the Cemetery with the Church (Groblje Preko).

My mother even received flowers from one of the island's residents. The old gentleman was very sweet. 🌸🌼🌺

At the end of the day, we settled among the rocks and sand on the shore, where we rested our feet in the icy waters while enjoying the stunning view of Otok Galevac Island.

21/05 - Exploring Zadar

Today was the day to explore the city of Zadar.

We started with the Morske orgulje (Sea Organ), a unique architectural installation that produces music naturally with the movement of the waves.

The music created by the waves on this structure is simply incredible. It's a symphony. 🎶🎵

After appreciating the view and the sound, we strolled through the Historic Center (Povijesni centar Zadra) and visited several churches: the Church of St. Donatus (Crkva sv. Donata), the Cathedral of St. Anastasia (Katedrala sv. Stošije), the Church of St. Simeon (Crkva sv. Šime), the Church of St. Mary (Crkva sv. Marije), and the Church of St. Francis (Crkva sv. Frane). Unfortunately, they were all closed, frustrating my mother's plans to visit a church on her first trip to Europe.

After that, we explored other magnificent points of Zadar:

  • Walked along the Riva (Šetnja Rivaom);
  • Saw the Zadar Walls (Zidina Zadra);
  • Visited Queen Jelena Madijevka Park;
  • People's Square (Narodni trg);
  • and the famous Široka ul. street, known as Kalelarga.

We ended the day at the Five Wells Square (Trg Pet Bunara), where the Land Gate and Captain's Tower are located.

We walked more than 13 kilometers. 🚶‍♀️🚶🏽🚶‍♀️🚶‍♂️

And to finish the day we had dinner and went to Pozdrav suncu (Greeting to the Sun), an interactive art project next to the Sea Organ consisting of solar panels that light up at night, creating a stunning light show.

22/05 - Boat Trip to Kornati and Telascica

Today was a day of maritime adventure!

We boarded the Gostilje, a wooden boat, and headed to Kornat Island, where we enjoyed a delicious barbecue lunch with Croatian wine.

Afterward, we explored Kornati National Park and Telascica Nature Park. We had time to swim, hike, and take photos of the beautiful landscapes.

We visited a viewpoint with steep cliffs and the salt lake Mir, where we took a picturesque walk and a refreshing dip.

Mental note: never forget to bring a jacket, as the wind on the boat was very strong! 🌬️🛥️

23/05 - Beach Day

Today was a relaxing day at the beaches of Zadar.

We started at Kolovare Beach (Plaža Kolovare), one of the most popular and about a 24-minute walk from the city center.

Then, we visited Borik Beach (Plaža Borik), known for its crystal-clear waters and good tourist facilities.

We spent the day sunbathing, diving into the cold waters, having a lot of laughs, and drinking some coktails.

It is essential to apply sunscreen and drink plenty of water.

the waters of the Adriatic Sea in the Zadar region are extremely cold, but worth a dip. 🤿👙🌊

24/05 - Time to say goodbye

Unfortunately, it was time to return home.

We took the bus to the airport at 4:05 in the morning. After a few hours of waiting, our flight left at 9:05, and we returned to Berlin, carrying many unforgettable memories.

We hope this little account inspires others to explore Zadar with the same joy and adventure we had!

Until the next trip! 🌍✈️❤️
